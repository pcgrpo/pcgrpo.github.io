PC-GRPO post-trains VLMs using supervision-free visual puzzles, a difficulty-aware curriculum, and reasoning–answer consistency for stronger, more reliable reasoning.

Overview

Puzzle Curriculum GRPO (PC-GRPO) is a supervision-free RL recipe that improves chain-of-thought visual reasoning in VLMs without labels or external verifiers. It trains models on three self-supervised “puzzle” tasks (PatchFit, Rotation, Jigsaw with partial credit) using a difficulty-aware curriculum that avoids flat rewards and vanishing advantages. By tracking and boosting Reasoning–Answer Consistency (RAC), PC-GRPO yields more consistent reasoning, better training stability, and open-source SoTA among supervision-free GRPO models on Qwen-7B/3B across multiple benchmarks.

We show that Nerfies can turn casually captured selfie photos/videos into deformable NeRF models that allow for photorealistic renderings of the subject from arbitrary viewpoints, which we dub "nerfies". We evaluate our method by collecting data using a rig with two mobile phones that take time-synchronized photos, yielding train/validation images of the same pose at different viewpoints. We show that our method faithfully reconstructs non-rigidly deforming scenes and reproduces unseen views with high fidelity.
